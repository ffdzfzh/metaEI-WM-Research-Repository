Fig. 3 | User-instruction-driven prediction, decision and metasurface execution in WM-metaEI.
a, Execution pipeline triggered by a region-level user instruction, such as enhancing the signal quality in a specified room. The system resolves the semantic target through the reconstructed digital twin, identifies the relevant transmitter, IMS and target region, and converts the instruction into a physically defined EM control task. b, Prediction–inversion procedure used to compute the metasurface coding pattern. Dominant ray paths covering the target region are first extracted by ray tracing and spatial filtering to form a region-specific ray map. The desired target field is then back-projected to the metasurface aperture according to EM time-reversal symmetry, and a modified Gerchberg–Saxton procedure converts the resulting aperture field into a reflection phase distribution. After subtracting the incident phase estimated by the ray-tracing engine, the continuous phase profile is discretized into a 1-bit coding matrix for metasurface control. Forward field synthesis is subsequently used to verify the predicted redistribution of the EM field before physical execution. c, Representative metasurface hardware platforms used for experimental validation in indoor environments, including a 3.5-GHz PIN-diode-controlled metasurface with 32 × 48 elements, a 5-GHz PIN-diode-controlled metasurface with 16 × 16 elements, and a 5-GHz magnetic-switch-controlled metasurface with 14 × 15 elements. The same decision-and-execution workflow can be mapped onto these different IMS implementations by registering their operating frequency, aperture geometry, unit-cell arrangement and available phase states.
